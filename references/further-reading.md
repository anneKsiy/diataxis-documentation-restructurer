# Further Reading

This skill's `diataxis-model.md` reference is a condensed, practical summary tailored for restructuring work. For deeper understanding of the framework, these are the canonical sources:

## Primary Source

- **Diátaxis website:** https://diataxis.fr — Daniele Procida's full framework. The "Start here" page is the best entry point.
- **GitHub repo:** https://github.com/evildmp/diataxis-documentation-framework — The source files behind the website. Licensed CC-BY-SA 4.0.

## Key Pages (for when you need deeper context on a specific type)

When classifying a piece of content and the condensed reference isn't enough, these pages go deep on each type's characteristics, what to include, and what to exclude:

- **Tutorials:** https://diataxis.fr/tutorials/ — What makes a good tutorial, why explanation kills learning flow, the instructor-student relationship
- **How-to guides:** https://diataxis.fr/how-to-guides/ — Goal-orientation, the recipe analogy, why titles matter, writing from the user's perspective
- **Reference:** https://diataxis.fr/reference/ — Austerity, mirroring the codebase structure, keeping it in sync
- **Explanation:** https://diataxis.fr/explanation/ — Approaching the subject from multiple angles, opinions and perspectives, the "why" questions

## Boundary Disambiguation

These pages are particularly useful when content sits right on a boundary between two types:

- **Tutorials vs. how-to guides:** https://diataxis.fr/tutorials-how-to/ — The most commonly confused pair. Study vs. work is the key distinction.
- **Reference vs. explanation:** https://diataxis.fr/reference-explanation/ — When reference starts getting expansive and drifts into explanation.

## Workflow Philosophy

- **How to use Diátaxis:** https://diataxis.fr/how-to-use-diataxis/ — Procida's guidance on iterative, bottom-up improvement rather than top-down restructuring. Contains the important insight: "Don't create empty structures for tutorials/how-to guides/reference/explanation with nothing in them. Don't do that. It's horrible."
- **The compass:** https://diataxis.fr/compass/ — The two-question decision tool (action or cognition? acquisition or application?) for when intuition fails.
